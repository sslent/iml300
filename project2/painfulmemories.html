You would beg me to stop asking questions. Itai, you would say. Pain.

I learned early that the only way to be close to you was to do a craft together — quietly, hands busy, following a pattern. That was how you let me in. Side by side. Doing.

I have so many questions I never got to ask. I was too young then and thought you were the strongest person in the world. I didn't think I'd ever lose you. To me, you were immortal.

I carry all my questions the way you carried yours — working with my hands, hoping something will come through that doesn't need words.

Emily Dickinson wrote, "Tell all the truth but tell it slant." Some things are too painful to approach head on. You have to come at them sideways.

You taught me that some things can only be said slant.
