I heard pieces of your story from other family members so many times that you became something I could picture completely — the cave, the bombs, your brother, the sharp pain of starvation. Every detail is vivid even though I was never there and it happened long before I was born.   That's what a myth is, I think — a story so powerful that it passes down through generations and lives in the body.   You became a myth to me the way only real people can. Part of you will always live inside me.
